The Capacity Heatmap Modeler

Capacity Modeling — the course

Learn to build heatmaps that are trustworthy, not just colourful — for a person, a team, a company, or anything with capacity and demand over time. Every lesson reads at three depths; pick the one that suits the moment with the bar at the top and it follows you through the course.

Overview

Plain language, one picture, no maths. What it is and why it matters — a two-minute read.

In practice

The tool's real vocabulary, simple arithmetic, and worked examples for building a model.

In depth

The theory beneath, citations, edge cases, and where the model is a simplification.

The quizzes are applied: you're handed a situation and asked how to model it. They double as training and assessment material you can reuse in-product.

Lessons

  1. 1
    What is capacity modeling?

    The workbench idea: painting busy-ness over time, for anything that fills up.

  2. 2
    Reading the heatmap

    Cells, the blue→red ramp, Load Intensity, grey blackouts, roll-up views.

  3. 3
    Two modes: Subtractive vs Additive

    Do you have a real limit, or just relative heat? What sets the Reference.

  4. 4
    From raw to effective capacity

    Base capacity, Reducers, Blackouts, and the shrinkage that makes real room smaller.

  5. 5
    Modeling demand: consumers & load shapes

    Recurring vs Spanning consumers, load shapes, phases, and why load sums.

  6. 6
    Utilisation and the 100% trap

    Why an all-red heatmap is a warning: queueing, Little's Law, target bands.

  7. 7
    Composition & constraints

    Do sources add up, or does the weakest link win? Sum vs min, and the bottleneck.

  8. 8
    Scaling: person → team → company

    One model, every level. Dimensions, faceting, small multiples, rollup = summation.

  9. 9
    Capstone: model a scenario end-to-end

    Put it all together — build one complete Scenario, decision by decision.
